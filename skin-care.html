The power of Lavender

This legendary herb belongs to the mint family, but its taste and aroma are absolutely unique. The strong, crisp scent is described as a mix of mint and rosemary with a rich, floral twist. The long history of lavender as a beauty and medical treatment suggests that lavender oil is far more than just a pretty plant.

How Is Lavender Essential Oil Made?

Needless to say, lavender oil has always been a favorite among essential oil lovers. But how exactly do those flowers transform into their well used oil form? Lavender is usually hand harvested from the fields. The flowers are tied together in bunches and laid out to dry for several weeks. Once the flowers are sufficiently dried, it’s time to begin steam distillation.

What are the benefits?

With skin care entering the self care realm, and more of us who are enjoying a lavender skin care routines more as pamper time rather than a chore, lavender oil for skin is a match made in heaven. Lavender oil not only boosts potent anti-inflammatory properties, but is also ultra soothing for any skin type with a therapeutic scent.

Anti-inflammatory Superstar

The effects of lavender are known far and wide for managing stress and reducing anxiety. Not surprisingly, it offers the same calming benefits for your skin. You can use calming lavender oil for skin to help reduce redness and blotchy patches. Lavender oil’s anti-inflammatory properties make it great for soothing and healing skin that’s irritated or reddened from the sun, insect bites, or bacteria.
